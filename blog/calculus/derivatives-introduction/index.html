Derivatives are all about slope.

Slope = Change In Y
Change In X

It's easy to find the average slope between two points.

Change in X = 15, Change in Y = 24
Slope = 24/15

But how do we find the slope at a point?

Change in X = 0, Change in Y = 0
Slope = 0/0 = ???

That's why we have differential calculus.
How does it work? All we do is instead of having one point we have two points that are infitesimally close together. Though we can't divide by zero, We can by an infitesimally small number...

First, let's understand "derivative".

e.g. what does it mean for the "derivative" of x2 to be 2x?

  • The derivative of x2 is 2x
  • If we have a point on the x2 graph, then the slope of that point is equal to 2 times the x-coordinate of the graph.
  • e.g. (5,25) on the x2 graph has a slope of 2*5 = 10.

How to calculate derivatives?

Example: calculate the derivative of f(x) where f(x) = x^2

Slope = f(x+Δx)-f(x)/Δx
